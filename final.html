Understanding Article Similarity

Welcome to our study!
The aim of this study is to better understand which articles news readers find similar to one another. This study is part of the Master's research of Vegard Solberg at the University of Bergen, Norway, under supervision of Prof. Dr. Christoph Trattner and Dr. Alain Starke.

Please carefully read the procedure followed in this study to give your informed consent for voluntary participation. *


Procedure
You will first be asked to rate 13 articles pairs on how similar you perceive them to be on a scale from 1 to 5. You will also be asked to provide a rating based on how confident you are in the similarity rating you have provided, and how familiar you are with the articles in question. In the next section, you will be asked to provide some insight as to what you based your similarity ratings on. In the final section you will be asked some questions about your personal characteristics, and to indicate how frequently you read news articles online.


Duration
The study takes around 15 minutes to complete.


Consent
By clicking "Next", you accept and agree that you have read and understood this consent form. 


*  The collected information is used for writing scientific publications. Your Personal information is processed anonymously and it cannot be traced back to you. For questions about the study, please contact the exprimenter, Vegard Solberg: Vegard.Solberg@uib.no
